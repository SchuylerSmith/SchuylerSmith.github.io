I am currently a Ph.D. candidate, working towards a degree in Bioinformatics and Computation Biology at Iowa State University in Ames, Iowa.

A member of the GERMS lab, run by Dr. Adina Howe, in the department of Agriculture and Biosystems Engineering. Our research focuses on environmental microbiomes and metagenomes; predominantly soil, but also water-systems, manure, plant-interactions, and even flower nectar.

My research focuses heavily on data analysis and building tools for reproducible research. I enjoy designing experiments and answering questions through data, but I equally enjoy enabling other researchers to answer their own questions. This is why I put together tools and packages such as the Phylosmith package I have listed on the navigation bar, along with other you may find on my GitHub.

Outside of research I have an interest in strategy games and fantasy/sci-fi novels. My favorite game series of all time is the Civilization turn-based strategy series. Favorite books series: the Kingkiller Chronicle by Pattrick Rothfuss.

In recent years I have also been fortunate to travel to various parts of the world. The more exotic places were on birding trips with my father, like the background picture I am using that I took at the Canopy Tower in Panama.
